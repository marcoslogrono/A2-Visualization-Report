Subtheme: Speculation

Dataset: "Corporate ownership rates and owner occupancy rates in Boston neighborhoods, 2004-2024"

This dataset consists of corporate ownership rates and owner occupancy rates for different neighborhoods of Boston over a 20 year span (2004-2024). The dataset also contains US Census data describing the demographics of each neighborhood. However, it is important to note that the demographic data is not up to date (no change in demographic data from 2004-2024).

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How does the number of vacant units compare between the neighborhoods of Boston? I would like to explore a possible connection between corporate ownership rate and number of vacant units among the different neighborhoods.
  2. How has the corporate ownership rate of housing in Boston changed over the years? I thought of this question while reading "When Private Equity Becomes Your Landlord". I am interested to see if the trend described in the article can also be observed in Boston.
  3. Is there a relationship between corporate ownership and owner occupancy rates? This question comes from the idea of "absentee owners", people that buy housing units only for investment purposes. Can we observe a trend in Boston?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

In this analysis we have identified some correlations between rates of corporate ownership, owner occupancy, and vacant units for neighborhoods in Boston from 2004 to 2024. Particularly, it was shown that neighborhoods with higher percentages of vacant units were more likely to be owned by corporations and private firms, and these tended to be in areas in or adjacent to downtown Boston. Additionally, an inverse relationship between corporate ownership and owner occupancy was identified. Units in suburban neighborhoods are more likely to be owned by individuals that live there, whereas low owner occupancy is observed in corporately owned units near the city. Further analysis with additional datasets might look into how these practices have affected rent prices in Boston and which demographics are most affected.
